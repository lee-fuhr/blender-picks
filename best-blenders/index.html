Best blenders, ranked

If you want a blender that will still be running strong a decade from now and produces genuinely smooth results every time, the Vitamix 5200 is the one most reviewers keep coming back to. Its tall, narrow container and simple variable-speed dial give you more control and better consistency than almost anything else on the market. For most households willing to invest in a machine that won’t quit, this is the clear pick.

That said, the right blender for your kitchen depends on your budget and how you’ll actually use it. If you’re making a smoothie every morning or two per week, a mid-range Ninja model with automated presets can save you money without sacrificing much in performance. And if you’re mostly blending single-serve drinks, a personal blender like the NutriBullet Pro 900 is more convenient, easier to clean, and still surprisingly capable. The five models below cover these scenarios honestly.

Vitamix 5200 — best overall

Owners consistently praise the Vitamix 5200 for its relentless durability and the silky texture it produces, whether you’re making nut butters, frozen smoothies, or hot soup. The tall, narrow container encourages a strong vortex that pulls ingredients down into the blades, so you rarely need to stop and scrape sides. The simple ten-variable-speed dial and high-low toggle give you fine control, and it’s widely regarded as the gold standard for a reason. Just be aware it’s louder than many newer designs.

Vitamix A3500 — best premium / smart presets / touchscreen

If you want the absolute latest from Vitamix plus the convenience of automated programs, the A3500 is the top-tier choice. Its touchscreen panel includes five presets for smoothies, soups, frozen desserts, and more, which reviewers say deliver reliably consistent results at the press of a button. The aircraft-grade stainless steel blades and 2.2-peak-HP motor are essentially the same powerhouse you get in the 5200, but with added features like a built-in timer and variable speed with pulse. The self-cleaning cycle also genuinely simplifies cleanup, though the premium price is steep.

Ninja Professional Plus BN701 — best value

For a fraction of the price of a Vitamix, the BN701 delivers remarkably capable blending that owners say handles frozen fruit, ice, and greens without issue. The 1400-watt motor and Auto-iQ presets add a helpful layer of automation—one touch runs a smart cycle that pauses and changes speed to pull everything down into the blades. The 72-ounce pitcher is generous for family batches, and the stacked blade assembly creates a good vortex. The main trade-off: it’s noisier and less refined than premium blenders, and the plastic pitcher can scratch over time.

Blendtec Total Classic — best for tough blends

Reviewers often call the Total Classic the tank of the blender world. Its blunt, two-inch stainless steel blade is safer to handle than sharp blades and stays efficient years later. The square, wide-mouth jar has no center post, so ingredients drop directly onto the blade and get hammered into submission, making it exceptional for kale, frozen bananas, and even grinding grains. The pre-programmed cycles are simple and effective, and the motor is backed by a reliable warranty. It is, however, very loud, and the jar’s square shape can make emptying every last bit tricky.

NutriBullet Pro 900 — best simple personal blender for single smoothies

If you mainly make one smoothie at a time and want something that fits on your counter without taking over, the NutriBullet Pro 900 is a proven, affordable choice. The 900-watt motor and extractor blade handle leafy greens, frozen berries, and seeds into genuinely smooth drinks, and the simple twist-and-blend operation couldn’t be easier. Owners appreciate that the cups double as travel containers, and cleanup is quick with just a rinse. It’s not designed for hot soups, large batches, or ice crushing to snow, but for daily single servings it’s hard to beat for the price.
